Diploma in Information Technology (s69)

The Diploma of Information Technology is one of the four courses provided by the School of Computing in SP. In this course, Year 1 students are able to choose from three different specialisations in Semester 2, with the three being Software Development, User Experience as well as Immersive Simulation! Year 2 students can consider two different pathways, one being the normal education pathway where they continue to learn the different modules in a classroom setting. The other pathway is the Industry Now Curriculum (INC), INC provides an alternate learning programme to achieve curricular competencies by working on real-world industry projects.

What I Study

All students in School of Computing have common modules in Year 1 Semester 1. We have 7 modules in the first semester which are :

  1. Communicating for Project (Proposal) Effectiveness
  2. Critical and Analytical Thinking (CAT)
  3. Mathematics
  4. Education and Career Guidance 1: Personal Development
  5. Front-End Web Development
  6. Fundamentals of Computing
  7. Fundamentals of Programming

These modules are about 1.5-3 hours each session, and we spend approximately 25 hours per week in school. Mathematics is quite similar to E.Maths with some elements of A.Maths, so if you have a strong foundation in math, I'm sure this module will be easy for you! Fundamentals of Computing will basically cover the technical side of computers, teaching students computer networking concepts and more. Fundamentals of Programming teaches us students how to solve problems with Javascript. Front-End Web Development equips students with skills to develop front-end web applications using HTML and CSS. For the rest of the modules, they allow students to hone their presentation as well as proposal writing skills which will be beneficial to them in the future. If you are interested in any of the courses under the School of Computing and would like to get started with coding, some advice I would give would be to start learning Javascript, HTML and CSS first as these will be what you will learn under Fundamentals of Programming and Front-End Web Development!

Career Prospects

According to Singapore Polytechnic's website, graduates can look forward to job roles such as Applications Developer, Systems Analyst, IT Consulting Analysts and Business Analyst. Other job roles that are suitable, depending on the specialisations are UX Designer, UI Designer, Data Engineer, Data Analyst and Digital Marketing Executive. For me, I am interested in pursuing a career as a UX or UI Designer and I hope I'll be able to choose the User Experience elective as my specialisation in Year 1 Semester 2!

Systems Analyst

A systems analyst, also known as business technology analyst, is an information technology (IT) professional who specializes in analyzing designing and implementing information systems. Systems analysts assess the suitability of information systems in terms of their intended outcomes and liaise with end users, software vendors and programmers in order to achieve these outcomes. A systems analyst is a person who uses analysis and design techniques to solve business problems using information technology.

UI/UX Designer

User experience (UX) designers are responsible for creating an optimal experience for the user when they interact with a digital or physical product, such as a website or a coffee machine. User interaction (UI) designers are primarily concerned with how a user navigates through a digital product. UI/UX designers are responsible for overall user satisfaction with a product. Their priority is to continually look for ways to improve the product experience, even for bestselling products that have been on the market for years.

Mobile App Developer

Mobile app developer’s primary duty is to create, maintain, and implement the source code to develop mobile apps and mobile platform programs that meet the needs and requirements of the clients using the computer programming languages. Additionally, mobile application developers may also design prototype applications, provide the unit structure, and help the application development team or clients in the plans.

Website Developer

Web Developer is responsible for the coding, design and layout of a website according to a company's specifications. As the role takes into consideration user experience and function, a certain level of both graphic design and computer programming is necessary. Once a website has been created, a Web Developer will generally assist with the maintenance and upkeep of the website.
